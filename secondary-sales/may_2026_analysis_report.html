Decomposition of the −$14.74 AOV gap by category, ranked by contribution:

  1. next_course collapse — graduation-stage sales had AOV $282 in Apr, only $212 in May (−25%). Avg package fell from 33 to 24 lessons. This single type contributed the largest drag.
  2. Package mix shift — 25-32 lesson packages share fell from 9.6% → 4.7% (–$12.6 contribution). Package 41+ (48/80 lesson bundles) share fell from 5.5% → 3.6% (–$9.5). Partly offset by package 40 growing (+$11.3) and package 10 growing (+$3.6).
  3. Manager performance regressions — four managers (Sultan, Fatin, Amel, Bayu) saw AOV drops of 25–59% individually. Together they contributed about –$31 to the AOV change.

This is the answer.

next_course is normally a high-ticket transaction: AOV $258–284 for five months, avg package 32–34 lessons.

In May 1–17, next_course AOV crashed to $212 (−25% vs Apr) and avg package dropped to 24 lessons (−27% vs Apr's 33.5).

next_course share of sales also fell: from 33% (Apr) to 30% (May).

prolongation AOV actually IMPROVED in May ($162 → $174), and its share grew.

Mechanism: Students who graduate to a new course in May are choosing smaller starting packages than before. They used to start the new course with 32–34 lessons; now it's 24. This is either pricing policy, a course-mix change in who's graduating, or a sales conversation difference.

Insights & recommended next investigations

  1. Investigate next_course package-size policy in May. This is the single biggest leverage point. Check: (a) Was there a promo or default-package change for grad transactions? (b) Are managers proposing smaller starter packages on next-course sales? (c) Is the course-mix of graduating students different (e.g. more students graduating into Math Tutoring, which is 2×/week and tends toward smaller packages)?
  2. Audit Sultan, Fatin, Amel, Bayu individually for May. Sultan went from $244 / 29 sales (Apr) to $113 / 11 sales (May). Fatin from $213 / 43 to $152 / 18. These are not gentle dips. Check if it's their course-mix or their package-recommendation behavior.
  3. Upgrade share declining from 47% to 30% over 5 months is the structural trend behind everything else. This deserves its own investigation: are students less inclined to step up, or are they being offered the same package by default? Same-share growth from 25% to 38% suggests a habit of "renew what you had."
  4. Private group_type AOV erosion (−13% Dec→May) is a slow leak that's separate from the May crash. Worth a deeper look — Private students have $13–14 PPL (highest tier), so any package shrinkage here costs disproportionately.
  5. Confirm with full-month May data once available. The 1–17 window is fair-comparison, but the second half of May may behave differently (end-of-month spike historically). Re-run when full data is in.
  6. Things that are NOT broken: bundling (growing, AOV-positive), age mix (stable), PPL (stable), volume (sales per day held up in May), prolongation type (actually improved).
